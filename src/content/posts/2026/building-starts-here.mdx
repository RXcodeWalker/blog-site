I like building things.

Most of the projects here started with something that annoyed me or a question I couldn't answer. Instead of leaving it there, I started building. Some ideas turned into projects I still work on every day. Others didn't make it very far. Both taught me something, so I write about both.

<strong>Momentum OS</strong>

The project I spend the most time on is Momentum OS.

It started because I was frustrated with productivity apps. They were good at storing tasks, but they didn't help me understand why I kept putting those tasks off. Momentum is my attempt to solve that.

I'm building an AI system that learns how you work, adapts over time, and helps you stay consistent. A lot of my time goes into thinking about behavior, habits, psychology, and how software fits into all of that.

<strong>French AI</strong>

French has been part of my life for as long as I can remember. I started learning it when I was five years old. During first and second grade, I went to a bilingual school where half of every school day was in French. We learned subjects like maths and science in French, and we were encouraged to speak French throughout the day.

When I moved to an English-medium school in third grade, French became another subject instead of a language I used every day. Even so, those first few years gave me a strong foundation. My teachers have often told me that my accent sounds close to a native speaker, and I think a lot of that comes from being surrounded by the language so early.

As I got older, I noticed something else. Most students weren't struggling with grammar or vocabulary. They struggled with speaking. In many schools, especially here in India, French classes focus on reading, writing, and exam preparation. There isn't much time for conversation, especially in a classroom with twenty or thirty students. Speaking on the spot is a different skill, and most people don't get enough practice.

That's why I'm building French AI.

The goal is simple. I want students preparing for IGCSE, DELF, or anyone learning French on their own to have somewhere they can practise speaking whenever they want. They should be able to hold conversations, make mistakes, receive useful feedback, and improve without needing a teacher sitting beside them.

<strong>This website</strong>

This website started as a place to publish what I write. Somewhere along the way, building the site became a project in its own right.

I keep changing things because I enjoy making the experience better. Sometimes that means redesigning a page. Sometimes it means reorganising content so it's easier to find. Other times it's adding features I wish more websites had. The site you see today looks nothing like the first version, and I don't expect it to stay the same for long.

<strong>Why I'm writing about all of this</strong>

You'll find progress updates, design decisions, mistakes, ideas that never worked, and things I figured out after spending far too long trying to solve them.

I like writing while projects are still changing. Most people only see the finished product. I think the process is far more interesting.
